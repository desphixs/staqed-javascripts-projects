### Path Title
Handling Data: Search, Filter, and Display

### Path Overview
In this path, you will learn how to make your website handle real information. Instead of copying and pasting the exact same HTML code over and over for every single item on a page, you will learn how to use JavaScript to automatically create your layout from a list of data. You will also learn how to build a working search bar and filter buttons so users can instantly sort and interact with the content on the screen.

### Project Title
Build CineSync: A Movie App

### Project Narrative
You are going to build CineSync, a sleek, dark-themed movie database. You will start by setting up a list of movies using JavaScript and writing the logic to automatically display them as beautiful posters on the page. Then, you will bring the app to life by wiring up a search bar that updates the results instantly as you type, and a filter button to show only the top-rated films. Finally, you will learn how to upgrade the app to pull real, live movie data straight from the internet.

### Story Arc
Meet David. He loves watching movies, but he absolutely hates trying to figure out what to watch. Every time he opens a big streaming app, it takes forever to load, the menus are confusing, and it's impossible to just quickly search for something good. He spends more time scrolling than actually watching anything. 

He constantly wishes there was just a fast, clean app that let him search for movies and instantly filter out the bad ones. That is why he came up with the idea for CineSync. He designed a great-looking interface, but right now, it's just a static picture. The search bar doesn't type, and the buttons don't do anything.

That is where you come in. Your job is to write the JavaScript that powers the app. You need to take a raw list of movies, put them on the screen, and make the search and filter tools work perfectly so movie fans like David can finally find what they want to watch without the headache. Let's get to work.
